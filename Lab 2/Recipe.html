Mapo Tofu: Recipe


About Mapo Tofu

Mapo tofu is a well-known Chinese dish, specifically originating from the Szechuan province. It combines Chinese chili-bean paste with tofu, as well as minced pork.


Ingredients

  • 2 blocks of cotton tofu in bite-sized cubes
  • 250 grams of minced pork
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 tablespoon of finely chopped, fresh ginger
  • Half of 1 leek, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of Chinese chili-bean paste
  • Corn starch, 1 tablespoon
  • Sesame oil, 1 tablespoon

For Seasoning:

  • 1 cup of water
  • Miso paste, 3 tablespoons
  • Sake, 2 tablespoons
  • 1 teaspoon of chicken soup powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt

Instructions

  1. In a big bowl, combine the water, miso, sake, chicken soup powder, sugar, and salt, Stir until sugar dissolves, then set aside.
  2. Wrap tofu with paper towel and heat the tofu for 1 minute in the microwave. Drain any excess water and cut into small cubes.
  3. Heat sesame oil in a wok on low heat, add the garlic, ginger, leek, and chili-bean paste. Stir fry for 3 minutes, then add the minced pork and cook until the color changes.
  4. Pour the seasoning mixture into the wok and bring to a boil. Add the tofu cubes and simmer on medium heat for approximately 10 minutes.
  5. Mix the corn starch with 1 tbsp. of water and add it to the wok, stir gently until the sauce thickens and smoothens out. Remove from heat and plate.
  6. Add some garnish to the tofu with spring onion and serve with steamed rice.
